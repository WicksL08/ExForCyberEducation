Student Code of Conduct

Before participating in any CTF activity, read and understand these expectations. Ethical participation makes this research possible.

// purpose of this agreement

The goal of this agreement is to ensure all students understand how to:

  • Participate ethically and responsibly in all cybersecurity activities
  • Protect their own privacy and digital safety
  • Respect others and maintain a positive learning environment
  • Contribute to research in a safe and anonymous way
// participation guidelines

As a participant, I agree to:

Use my skills for good

I will not use what I learn to harm people, systems, or data. Cybersecurity knowledge is a responsibility.

Respect privacy

I will never access, share, or post personal or school information without explicit permission.

Play fair

I will complete challenges honestly without using outside tools, sharing flags, or using AI to find answers.

Stay within bounds

I will only test or explore within the CTFd environment provided. No unauthorized access or real-world probing.

Be respectful and supportive

I will help others learn and maintain a positive, inclusive environment for everyone in the class.

Ask for help when unsure

If I encounter something that feels wrong or confusing, I will notify my teacher or the project lead immediately.

// data & privacy notice

IRB Approved

This activity is part of an IRB-approved research study conducted through The University of Alabama.

Voluntary Participation

All participation is voluntary. Students may stop at any time without penalty or consequence.

Anonymous & Confidential

Student data is anonymous and confidential. No personal or identifying data is stored or published.

Aggregate Results Only

Results are analyzed in aggregate to improve cybersecurity education — never linked to individual students.

"Cybersecurity begins with integrity."

// for teachers & guardians

Teachers and guardians may review this agreement with students before participation begins. This document ensures everyone understands how to engage safely and ethically in cybersecurity learning activities.
