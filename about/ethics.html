Ethics & IRB Approval

Responsible cybersecurity education means doing the right thing with every skill, every system, and every piece of data.

IRB Approved · The University of Alabama · Protocol on file
// ethical principles

What Does "Ethical" Mean in This Context?

Ethics in cybersecurity is about doing the right thing even when no one is watching. Students are encouraged to:

  • Use their skills to protect systems — never to exploit them.
  • Respect privacy — never access or share information without permission.
  • Ask permission before testing or scanning any system.
  • Report vulnerabilities responsibly to a teacher or administrator.
  • Use cybersecurity challenges to learn, not to break rules.
"Our goal: to create responsible, informed, and ethical cybersecurity citizens."
// conduct standards

Participation Rules

✓ Allowed

  • Solving CTF challenges in the provided platform
  • Collaborating with classmates on challenges
  • Asking teachers or facilitators for hints
  • Reporting bugs or issues with challenges
  • Exploring learning materials and resources

✗ Not Allowed

  • Unauthorized hacking or system testing
  • Scraping or collecting personal information
  • Sharing or publishing challenge answers
  • AI-assisted cheating on assessments
  • Accessing systems outside the CTFd platform

All activities occur in controlled, simulated CTF environments where students can safely practice without affecting real systems.

// research standards

IRB & Research Ethics

This study has been reviewed and approved by the University of Alabama Institutional Review Board (IRB). All data is collected anonymously and used only for educational improvement and research analysis.

  • Participation is voluntary — you may stop at any time without penalty.
  • No identifying information is ever recorded.
  • Teachers act as facilitators only — not evaluators.
  • All results are reported in aggregate form to protect privacy.
  • Data is stored securely under UA research compliance policies.
