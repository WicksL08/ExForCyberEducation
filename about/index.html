// what is ctf4cyberlearning

Experiential Learning in Action

CTFforCyberLearning evaluates a Capture-the-Flag (CTF)-based experiential model for high school cybersecurity education across Alabama, grounded in the CTFforCyberLearning instructional model.

By connecting real-world cyber skills to Alabama's K–12 Computer Science Standards, we bridge the gap between classroom theory and hands-on technical practice — inspiring the next generation of cybersecurity professionals.

  • Grounded in experiential learning theory
  • Aligned to Alabama K–12 CS standards
  • Designed for high school classrooms
  • Fully IRB-approved and anonymous
  • Supported by The University of Alabama

// how it works

How CTFforCyberLearning Works

Students, teachers, and researchers each play an important role. Here's the process from start to finish.

01
📋

Complete Consent

Students and parents/guardians complete consent and assent forms before any participation begins.

02
📝

Take the Pre-Test

Students complete a baseline knowledge assessment via Qualtrics to measure starting cybersecurity understanding.

03
🔬

Explore the Learning Lab

Students watch tutorials and review vocabulary across three modules: OSINT, Cryptography, and Password Security.

04
🚩

Solve CTF Challenges

Students apply knowledge by solving real cybersecurity challenges on the secure CTFd platform, capturing flags for each solution.

05
💬

Reflect & Discuss

Teachers facilitate guided debrief discussions connecting challenge experiences back to real-world cybersecurity concepts.

06
📊

Post-Test & Feedback

Students complete a post-assessment and anonymous survey. Teachers submit observation feedback to the researcher.

// why ctf-based learning

Traditional Learning vs. CTFforCyberLearning

CTF-based learning transforms passive instruction into active problem-solving — here's how it compares to traditional classroom approaches.

Category Traditional Learning CTFforCyberLearning
Instruction StyleLecture-driven, teacher-centeredHands-on, student-driven exploration
Student RolePassive listener and note-takerActive problem solver and analyst
EngagementLimited — reading and memorizationHigh — gamified, challenge-based tasks
Skill ApplicationIsolated vocabulary and theoryIntegrated technical and ethical thinking
AssessmentTests on memorized contentPre/post assessments + live challenge performance
Teacher RoleInstructor and evaluatorFacilitator and guide — not grader
Career ConnectionAbstract or indirectReal analyst skills practiced directly

// research ethics & privacy

Research Ethics & Data Privacy

This study has been reviewed and approved by the University of Alabama Institutional Review Board (IRB). Participant privacy and ethical conduct are the foundation of this research.

🔒

Voluntary & Anonymous

All student participation is completely voluntary. Responses are kept fully anonymous throughout the study.

👩‍🏫

Teacher as Facilitator

Teachers guide and support — they do not evaluate or grade student participation in research activities.

📊

Qualtrics Data Collection

Pre/post assessments and surveys are administered anonymously through the secure Qualtrics platform.

📋

Aggregate Reporting Only

All findings are reported in aggregate. No individual student is ever identified in research outputs.

📝

Informed Consent

Students and parents/guardians must complete consent and assent forms before any participation begins.

🏛️

IRB Oversight

The study protocol has been reviewed and approved by the UA Institutional Review Board to protect all participants.
