What Students Will Experience

The Cyber Security Learning Module consists of three sequential modules. Click each tab to see what happens in that module.

Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)

Students learn ethical online investigation, information verification, and digital footprint analysis using publicly available tools and sources.

  • Mini-lesson: What is OSINT and how is it used ethically?
  • Tutorial videos on search tools and verification techniques
  • CTF Challenges: Find the Bean, World Traveler, Vanished Motto
  • Debrief discussion on digital ethics and responsible investigation

Cryptography

Students explore encoding, encryption, and classical cipher techniques — learning how information is hidden, protected, and decoded.

  • Mini-lesson: Encoding vs. encryption vs. hashing
  • Tutorial videos on Base64, substitution ciphers, and decoding
  • CTF Challenges: The Secret Scroll, The Hidden Codebook, The Headmaster's Secret
  • Debrief discussion on real-world encryption applications

Password Security

Students investigate how passwords are stored, cracked, and strengthened — learning about hashing, salting, rainbow tables, and strong password creation.

  • Mini-lesson: How passwords are stored and why they get cracked
  • Tutorial videos on hashing, salting, and rainbow tables
  • CTF Challenges: How Long Until It's Gone, County Salt, Forgotten Coffee Shop Vibes
  • Debrief discussion on personal password hygiene and best practices
