// introducing this to your classroom

How to Get Students Ready

🗣️ Opening Discussion Prompts

Use these before Session 1 to activate curiosity:

  • "Have you ever wondered how hackers actually break into systems?"
  • "What information about yourself is publicly available online right now?"
  • "What makes a password strong — and why does it matter?"
  • "If you could design a cybersecurity challenge, what would it look like?"

🧊 Ice Breaker — Digital Footprint

Ask students to spend 2 minutes writing every app, website, or platform they used in the last 24 hours. Then discuss:

"Who can see that information? What could someone figure out about you from that list?"

This sets up the OSINT module perfectly and gets students thinking like analysts immediately.

📋 Suggested Classroom Setup

  • One device per student (laptop or Chromebook preferred)
  • Seat students in pairs or groups of 2–3 for collaboration
  • Display the CTFd URL and Learning Lab link on the board
  • First 10 minutes = mini-lesson; last 5 minutes = whole-class debrief

💬 What to Tell Students & Parents

  • "This is a real UA research study — your input helps improve CS education statewide."
  • "All data is completely anonymous — nothing is tied to you personally."
  • "A CTF is like a cybersecurity scavenger hunt — solve puzzles, earn points."
  • "Participation is 100% voluntary — you can stop at any time."

📝 Mini-Lesson Talking Points

Session 1 — OSINT: Analysts use only public info — no hacking. Everything online leaves a trace.

Session 2 — Cryptography: Decode a Caesar cipher on the board by hand before opening CTFd. Make it tactile first.

Session 3 — Passwords: Ask "How does a website know your password without storing it?" — introduce hashing as a one-way lock.

🔒 Ethics Reminder for Students

  • All work must stay within the CTFd environment only
  • No unauthorized access or real-world system testing
  • Do not share flags or answers with other students
  • Use skills to protect — never to harm

How to Structure Each Session

1
0 – 5 min (Session 1 only)

Consent & Pre-Test Check

Confirm all consent forms are submitted. Have students complete the pre-test before anything else.

2
5 – 15 min

Mini-Lesson

Introduce the session topic (OSINT, Cryptography, or Password Security) using the talking points above. Keep it conversational — ask questions, don't lecture.

3
15 – 45 min

CTF Challenge Time

Students log into CTFd and work through challenges. Circulate, give hints if stuck, but don't give answers. Encourage collaboration within pairs.

4
45 – 50 min

Debrief & Reflection

Bring the class back together. Ask: "What did you find? What surprised you? How does this connect to real life?"

5
Final session only

Post-Test & Feedback

Reserve the last 10 minutes for students to complete the post-test and feedback form. Submit your own teacher feedback form as well.
