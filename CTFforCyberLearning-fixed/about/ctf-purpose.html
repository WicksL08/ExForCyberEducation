The Purpose of Capture the Flag

CTFs turn theory into practice. Instead of reading about cybersecurity concepts, students apply them to real-world scenarios — building skills through exploration, not memorization.

// traditional vs. ctf learning

// what students learn

The Three Learning Modules

Module 01

OSINT

Open-Source Intelligence — ethical online investigation, information verification, and digital footprints.

Module 02

Cryptography

Encoding, substitution ciphers, and decoding hidden messages — the fundamentals of protecting information.

Module 03

Password Security

Hashing, salting, rainbow tables, and password strength — understanding how credentials are protected and cracked.

Skills Students Develop

Critical Thinking Digital Ethics Collaboration Cyber Awareness Problem Solving Technical Reasoning Research Skills Career Readiness
// how it works

The CTF Learning Flow

01

Complete consent forms — students and parents/guardians sign the required forms before the study begins.

02

Take the pre-test — a short anonymous assessment to measure prior knowledge before challenges begin.

03

Complete the Learning Lab — review lesson materials, vocabulary, and tutorials for each module.

04

Solve CTF challenges — hosted on the CTFd platform; each captured flag represents a learning milestone.

05

Take the post-test — measure growth and reflect on what was learned through the experience.

"When students play, explore, and think like analysts — they learn more deeply."
