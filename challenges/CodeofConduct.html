Student Code of
Conduct & Agreement

Before participating in any CTF challenge or activity, please read and understand the following expectations. Cyber Security begins with integrity.

// purpose

Purpose of This Agreement

This agreement ensures all students understand how to participate ethically and responsibly in all CTFforCyberLearning activities. By following these guidelines, students help create a trustworthy, inclusive, and professional learning space.

// ethical participation guidelines

As a Participant, I Agree To:

Use my Cyber Security skills for good

I will not use what I learn to harm people, systems, or data in any way.

Respect privacy

I will never access, share, or post personal or school information without permission.

Play fair

I will complete challenges honestly without using outside tools or AI to find answers.

Follow instructions

I will only test or explore within the CTF environment provided — nowhere else.

Be respectful and supportive

I will help others learn and maintain a positive environment for everyone.

Ask for help

If I encounter something I don't understand or that feels wrong, I will notify the researcher immediately.

// data & privacy

Data & Privacy Notice

This activity is part of an IRB-approved research study conducted through The University of Alabama.

Voluntary

All participation is completely voluntary. You may stop at any time without penalty.

Anonymous

Student data is fully anonymous and confidential. No personal data is stored or published.

Aggregate Results

Results are analyzed only in aggregate form to improve Cyber Security education.

IRB Approved

This study has been reviewed and approved by the UA Institutional Review Board.

"Cyber Security begins with integrity."
